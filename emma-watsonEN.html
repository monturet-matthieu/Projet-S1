Emma Watson : an icon of the 21st century

Born on April 15, 1990 in Paris, Emma Watson is best known for her acting career (Hermione Granger in the Harry Potter saga). However, she has done other prestigious jobs, such as model or muse for the Burberry and Lancôme brands. But also, of course, spokesperson for the feminist movement HeForShe.

Emma Watson speaking for HeForShe

Carrier

Her career began at the age of 10 and lasted until 2011 to follow all Harry Potter as Hermione Granger. During this period, she quickly became a star and took part in many different events such as fashion shows and appeared on the front page of many magazines. She will mark a break in her film career before resuming it in 2017 with The Circle and La Belle et la Bête.

A new figure of feminism

She is heavily involved in humanitary aid, but especially in the defence of women’s rights. She is particularly active in the HeForShe Campaign, where she advocates for gender equality. Appointed UN Women Goodwill Ambassador in 2014, which will allow her to deliver a famous speech at the United Nations in 2016. In January 2018, during the emergence of Time’s Up, she said that what matters to her is to make sure it’s a global movement .

She stressed the need for women and men from all walks of life ” to be involved, as sexual harassement occurs everywhere. She also makes several donations to associations and funds such as Justice and Equality, to which she donates €1 million. More recently, she became a board member of Kering, a French luxury group since June 2020.

Focus on the speech

Following her appointment as UN Women Goodwill Ambassador, Emma Watson is offered a golden opportunity. She is invited to give a speech at the United Nations on September 20, 2014 to support the HeForShe campaign project, but also to address the feminist issue in general. She denounced sexual assaults on English university campuses, but she also wanted as many people as possible, women and men, to mobilize for the cause. That is why, while dedicating the feminist cause, which is too often associated with the hatred of men , she invites those who can act to do so.

In addition, she stressed that no country in the world offered equal income for women and men. It also raised the issue of education, particularly in Africa, where girls were unable to receive a good level of education because of lack of resources and mobilization. In conclusion, she invites all of us to come forward to make our ideas heard within the HeForShe movement. She ends with a simple question: If I don’t do it, who will? If I don’t do it now, when? . This powerful sentence is still valid, despite the efforts she and the organisations she supports are making.
